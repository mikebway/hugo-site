Al-Andalus was the Arabic name for the Muslim-ruled territories of the Iberian Peninsula. From 711 to 719, it expanded
from the southern coast to nearly all of what is now Spain and Portugal, reaching into southern France. The northern
Christian kingdoms eventually overpowered the southern Muslim states, with the [Emirate of Granada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Granada)
the last to fall in 1492. Despite the passage of five hundred years and the brutal expulsion and suppression of Jews
and Muslims by the Spanish Inquisition, Arab and Amazigh (Berber) DNA still permeates Spain today, literally and culturally.
